3<appendix id="sourcerepo">
4 <title>The Source Code Repository</title>
5
6 <para>
7  The <productname>PostgreSQL</productname> source code is stored and managed
8  using the <productname>Git</productname> version control system. A public
9  mirror of the master repository is available; it is updated within a minute
10  of any change to the master repository.
11 </para>
12
13 <para>
14  Our wiki, <ulink
15  url="https://wiki.postgresql.org/wiki/Working_with_Git"></ulink>,
16  has some discussion on working with Git.
17 </para>
18
19 <sect1 id="git">
20  <title>Getting the Source via <productname>Git</productname></title>
21
22  <para>
23   With <productname>Git</productname> you will make a copy of the entire code repository
24   on your local machine, so you will have access to all history and branches
25   offline. This is the fastest and most flexible way to develop or test
26   patches.
27  </para>
28
29  <procedure>
30   <title>Git</title>
31
32   <step>
33    <para>
34     You will need an installed version of <productname>Git</productname>, which you can
35     get from <ulink url="https://git-scm.com"></ulink>. Many systems already
36     have a recent version of <application>Git</application> installed by default, or
37     available in their package distribution system.
38    </para>
39   </step>
40
41   <step>
42    <para>
43     To begin using the <productname>Git</productname> repository, make a clone of the official mirror:
44
45<programlisting>
46git clone https://git.postgresql.org/git/postgresql.git
47</programlisting>
48
49     This will copy the full repository to your local machine, so it may take
50     a while to complete, especially if you have a slow Internet connection.
51     The files will be placed in a new subdirectory <filename>postgresql</filename> of
52     your current directory.
53    </para>
54   </step>
55
56   <step>
57    <para>
58     Whenever you want to get the latest updates in the system, <command>cd</command>
59     into the repository, and run:
60
61<programlisting>
62git fetch
63</programlisting>
64    </para>
65   </step>
66  </procedure>
67
68  <para>
69   <productname>Git</productname> can do a lot more things than just fetch the source. For
70   more information, consult the <productname>Git</productname> man pages, or see the
71   website at <ulink url="https://git-scm.com"></ulink>.
72  </para>
